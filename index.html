About me

I currently live in Chicago, IL.
I am enrolled in the MS in Computational Analysis and Public Policy at the University of Chicago.
Before this, I worked as a Research Assistant at the Urban Informatics Lab at the Indian Institute for Human Settlements. I mainly worked with public data, trying to glean more information from it, come up with methods to fill data gaps and turn it into publically consumable media. I looked at migration patterns across India, came up with novel ways of projecting Indian populations (national, state, district and city) and looked at health indicators across underserved communities.
In 2019, I graduated from BITS Pilani, Goa with a major in Electrical Engineering and a minor in Politics, Economics and Philosophy. I am interested in working on inter-disciplinary research between STEM and Public Policy.
Previously, I have worked as a Data Science Intern at Genpact and as a Research Intern at the North Eastern Space Applications Center, Department of Space. I am also fond of sports, sports analytics, trivia, film, comics and graphic design.

Projects

  • Developed a Shiny app (R) dashboard to explore domestic migration patterns across education, reason of movement, gender etc. built from national datasets such as the Census of India, 2011
  • Created national level estimates of district-to-district in-migration and out-migration to bridge data availability and quality gaps in publicly available datasets to further organisation wide understanding of migration parameters
  • Analyzed publicly available and confidential government datasets for the Rajasthan Chief Minister's office on rethinking urban employment and reframing social security and government benefits in the aftermath of the COVID-19 pandemic
  • Guided the data analysis effort for an International Labour Organisation project studying trends in domestic worker hiring involving a 12,000+ response multi-city survey
  • Investigated the discrepancies in child health indicators across Karnataka through the National Family Health Survey (DHS)
  • Presented the preliminary analysis at a webinar with over 200 attendees, to a panel with experts from UNICEF and NITI-Aayog
  • Predicted future fertility rates, mortality rates, and migration numbers to 2051 for 5-year projection intervals employing Bayesian hierarchical models; then applied outputs to age-sex cohorts to generate population pyramids every 5 years
  • Produced a confidential district level risk analysis report for the Govt of India in March 2020 for the COVID-19 pandemic exploring the spread potential, the impacts and the response capacity
  • Wrote the section on migration for a policy brief for the XV Finance Commission of India on Urban Infrastructure and Resilience
  • Designed, prepared content for, and taught sessions focusing on understanding Indian public data and other data skills in the Data Skill Lab as a faculty member of the Urban Fellows Programme
  • Proposed, created, and deployed an internal system on AWS built on Flask in Python for the admissions process of the Urban Fellows Programme. Handled over 20 internal graders concurrently for over 600 applications
